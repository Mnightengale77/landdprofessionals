Always arrive early,professionally dressed, and have your updated resume with you.

In this video, we demonstrated a bad interview and a good interview. In the bad interview we showed how not to act when you have an interview. In the good interview we not only showcased how to act but also demonstrated the STAR method being used in an interview.

Tips for interview preparation

  • Have your clothes ready
  • Give yourself extra travel time
  • Visualize the interview. Picture yourself as poised, confident, articulate.
  • Make a list of your strengths and weakness and practice how you will articulate them.
  • Write out answers to the questions you don’t want to be asked. It will help alleviate your anxiety.

STAR method explanation

The STAR method is a job interview technique used by interviewers to gather all relevant information about a specific capability that the job requires. S is for situation. It describes the situation you were in such as what job you had or if you were in school at the time. T is for Task. This describes what you had to accomplish such as dealing with a rude customer. A is for Action. These are the actions you took to handle the task. R is for Result. This describes the outcome of your actions. If you dealt with a rude customer calmly did you get them to calm down and buy something? Answering questions using this method is said to have a higher degree of predictability of the future on-the-job performance than a regular interview answer.

  • S- Situation
  • T- Task
  • A- Actions
  • R- Result
